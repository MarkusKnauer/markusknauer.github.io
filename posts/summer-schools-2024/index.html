This summer, I had the incredible opportunity to attend two outstanding summer schools that significantly expanded my knowledge in machine learning and AI. Here’s my experience from both events.

ELLIS Probabilistic Machine Learning Summer School - Cambridge Link to heading

The ELLIS Probabilistic Machine Learning Summer School in Cambridge was an intensive deep dive into the foundations and cutting-edge developments in probabilistic machine learning. The program featured world-class lecturers covering topics from Bayesian inference to modern probabilistic models.

Key Highlights: Link to heading

  • Bayesian Deep Learning: Advanced techniques for uncertainty quantification in neural networks
  • Variational Inference: Modern variational methods and their applications
  • Gaussian Processes: Theory and practical implementations
  • Probabilistic Programming: Hands-on sessions with modern frameworks
  • Causal Inference: Probabilistic approaches to understanding causality

The Cambridge setting provided an inspiring academic atmosphere, and the networking opportunities with fellow researchers from around the world were invaluable.

ELLIS Cambridge Certificate

OxML Summer School - Oxford: Representation Learning & Generative AI Link to heading

The OxML Summer School at Oxford focused on Representation Learning & Generative AI, covering the latest advances in how machines learn meaningful representations and generate new content.

Key Topics Covered: Link to heading

  • Foundation Models: Understanding transformer architectures and large language models
  • Diffusion Models: Theory and applications in image and text generation
  • Variational Autoencoders: Advanced VAE architectures and applications
  • Graph Neural Networks: Representation learning on structured data
  • Self-Supervised Learning: Modern approaches to learning without labels
  • Multimodal Learning: Bridging vision, language, and other modalities

The hands-on workshops provided practical experience with state-of-the-art models, and the discussions with leading researchers in the field were particularly enlightening.

Reflections and Takeaways Link to heading

Both summer schools offered complementary perspectives on modern machine learning:

  • ELLIS Cambridge provided deep theoretical foundations in probabilistic modeling, which are crucial for understanding uncertainty and robustness in AI systems
  • OxML Oxford showcased the latest in generative modeling and representation learning, highlighting the rapid pace of innovation in AI

The combination of rigorous theory from Cambridge and cutting-edge applications from Oxford has given me a well-rounded view of the current state and future directions of machine learning research.

Looking Forward Link to heading

The knowledge and connections gained from these summer schools will undoubtedly influence my future research directions. The insights into probabilistic machine learning will inform my work on interactive robot learning, while the exposure to generative AI opens up exciting possibilities for multimodal robotic applications.

I’m grateful for these learning opportunities and look forward to applying these insights in my ongoing research projects.
